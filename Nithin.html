         Web development involves creating, building, and maintaining websites. It encompasses everything from the layout and design of a webpage to its functionality, performance, and security. There are two main types of web development: front-end and back-end.
Front-end development focuses on the parts of a website that users interact with directly, like the layout, design, buttons, and navigation. This typically involves languages like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, which control the structure, style, and interactive behavior of the site.
Back-end development refers to the server-side of the website. It deals with databases, server logic, and application programming interfaces (APIs). Common back-end languages include PHP, Python, Ruby, and frameworks like Node.js.
Modern web development also includes full-stack development, where developers work on both the front-end and back-end.
Web developers ensure that websites are fast, responsive, and secure. They also optimize them for search engines (SEO) and integrate content management systems (CMS) like WordPress or Drupal.
With the growth of the internet, web development has become a crucial skill, powering millions of websites, applications, and online services worldwide.
